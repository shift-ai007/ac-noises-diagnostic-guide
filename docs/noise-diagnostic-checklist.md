# AC Noise Diagnostic Checklist

Use this checklist when you hear an unusual sound from your AC system. Work through it top to bottom.

## Step 1: Identify the Source

- [ ] Is the noise coming from the **indoor unit** (air handler)?
- [ ] Is the noise coming from the **outdoor unit** (condenser)?
- [ ] Is the noise coming from the **ductwork**?
- [ ] Is the noise coming from the **thermostat area**?

## Step 2: Characterize the Sound

- [ ] Banging or clanking (metallic, rhythmic)
- [ ] Screeching or squealing (high-pitched)
- [ ] Buzzing (electrical, continuous)
- [ ] Clicking (repeated, system won't start)
- [ ] Hissing (continuous, pressurized air escaping)
- [ ] Rattling (loose, intermittent)
- [ ] Gurgling or bubbling (liquid sounds)

## Step 3: Safe DIY Checks

- [ ] Check and replace air filter
- [ ] Check thermostat batteries
- [ ] Inspect outdoor unit for visible debris (twigs, leaves)
- [ ] Check that all supply vents are open and unobstructed
- [ ] Pour 1/4 cup vinegar down condensate drain line
- [ ] Listen for changes after each step

## Step 4: When to Stop and Call a Pro

Stop DIY troubleshooting immediately if you notice:
- [ ] Ice forming on any part of the system
- [ ] Water leaking from the indoor unit
- [ ] Burning smell
- [ ] Electrical sparking or arcing
- [ ] System trips the circuit breaker

## Step 5: Information to Have Ready

When calling for service, note:
- [ ] Exact location of the noise
- [ ] Type of noise (use the categories above)
- [ ] When it started
- [ ] Whether it's continuous or intermittent
- [ ] Your system's age and last maintenance date
- [ ] Any recent changes (thermostat adjustment, filter change, storm)

## Monthly Prevention Tasks (South Florida)

- [ ] Replace air filter (every 30-60 days in summer)
- [ ] Flush condensate drain with vinegar
- [ ] Clear debris from around outdoor unit (2 ft clearance)
- [ ] Check thermostat is reading correctly
- [ ] Listen for any new sounds during startup
